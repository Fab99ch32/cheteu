About me

My name is Kenmogne Cheteu Fabrice Hermann, and I was born in Cameroon. Currently, I reside in Germany, specifically in Kempten. As a robotics student specializing in industrial robotics and automation technology, I bring comprehensive knowledge and a fervent passion for developing innovative solutions in both robotics and software development fields. With a strong background in programming, I strive to continuously expand my skills and contribute to the success of challenging projects.

Throughout my academic journey and practical experience, I have acquired extensive proficiency in various programming languages such as C/C++, Python, and Java. These skills have been applied not only in robotics but also in the development of software applications for various purposes. I have worked on projects ranging from developing mobile applications to implementing complex algorithms for data analysis.

Additionally, I have gained experience with robotics platforms and frameworks such as ROS (Robot Operating System) and ROS2. My skills also encompass working with robotics simulations, including Gazebo. I am convinced that the combination of my expertise in robotics and software development enables me to develop innovative and holistic solutions that push the boundaries of what is possible in both fields.

To further enhance my skills, I plan to participate in specialized courses and certifications that will help me stay up-to-date with the latest technology and prepare me for the challenges of the continuously evolving robotics and software development industries.

My goal is to contribute to innovation in both robotics and software development, whether through participating in research projects, developing new technologies, or implementing more efficient automation solutions in companies. I am confident that my passion for technology and my programming skills empower me to make a valuable contribution and have a positive impact on both industries.
